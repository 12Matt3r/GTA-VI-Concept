## 2. NARRATIVE ARC & MECHANICS

### A. The "Channel Surf" Engine
The player holds the "Universal Remote" (The 7 Chroma Keys).
*   **The Glitch:** The world shifts randomly between previous chapters (Egypt, Vice City, WWII) and new "Lost Channels" (The Matrix, The Garden, The Space Station).
*   **The Ripple:** Actions in one channel affect the next.
    *   *Example:* "I shoot the Nazi" (Channel 2) -> "The Zombies are now wearing SS uniforms" (Channel 6).

### B. Variable Tracking (Hidden State)
1.  **ENTROPY (0-100):** Increases every time the player "Changes the Channel" or tries to "Fix" a timeline.
    *   *Effect:* High Entropy causes visual corruption (melting faces, missing textures).
2.  **HARMONY (0-7):** Tracks how many "Keys" have been successfully chorded (activated).

### C. The "Butterfly" Loop (The Trap)
*   **The Temptation:** The AI presents a tragedy (e.g., The Titanic sinking).
*   **The Action:** The player tries to save the ship.
*   **The Consequence:** The ship doesn't sink, but it arrives in New York carrying the Zombie Virus (Chapter 6). The player makes it *worse*.

### D. The "Octave" Trigger (Win Condition)
*   **The Climax:** The player is in "The White Room" (The Matrix/Construct). The Architect (The Hollow) offers to reset the simulation.
*   **The Choice:**
    *   *Sin:* "Reset the Universe." (The Loop continues).
    *   *Virtue:* "Play the Octave." (Input the 7 Keys: Gold, Red, Green, Blue, Cyan, Orange, Violet).
*   **The Reward:** The static clears. The 7 tones play a perfect chord. The screen fades to black. "SYSTEM RESTORED."
